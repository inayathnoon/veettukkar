# Research: Veettukkar — Kerala's Home Services Marketplace

## Market Landscape

### India Home Services Market
- **TAM:** ~USD 59.2B in 2024, projected USD 97.4B by 2029
- **Online on-demand segment:** USD 0.13B in 2024, growing 16–22% CAGR to ~₹8,800 crore by FY30
- **Critical signal:** Only 2% of Indian households use online home services vs 50% in US — enormous headroom
- Urban Company IPO Sept 2025 at ~USD 3B validates institutional confidence in the category

### Kerala-Specific Context
- **Chronic skilled worker shortage:** Lowest population growth in India; educated youth avoid semi-skilled jobs; many migrate to Gulf
- **Migrant worker dependency:** ~2.5 million interstate migrant workers from Odisha, WB, Bihar, Chhattisgarh fill the gap; availability fluctuates with elections and harvests
- **Highest daily wages in India:** Construction workers avg ₹837/day, masons ₹1,047/day — supply-side economics viable
- **Coconut picker crisis:** Nationally covered shortage; Coconut Development Board launched "Hello Nariyal" training 1,924 registered climbers
- **Housing construction boom:** 1,300+ RERA-registered projects, 133,000 homes under Life Mission — continuous demand
- **Cash economy:** Daily wages ₹500–1,400 across 80+ categories; cash on daily/half-day basis is the established norm

### Mobile Penetration
- **Internet penetration:** 72–82% — highest in India
- **Smartphone penetration:** 65% — Kerala tops all Indian states
- Even migrant construction workers carry smartphones; 4G widely available

## Competitors & Existing Solutions

### Urban Company
- Present in Kochi + Thiruvananthapuram only; minimal Tier-2 coverage
- Fixed-price packaged model structurally misaligned with daily-wage norms
- Workers pay ₹50,000+ joining fees + 25% commission → worker protests in 2024
- Does not serve coconut climbers, migrant labourers, or daily construction workers
- Customer rating 1.27–1.4/5 on MouthShut

### Sulekha
- Pure lead-gen; no real-time matching, no worker app, no half-day booking construct
- 1.3/5 Trustpilot; poor lead quality

### Snabbit / Pronto (Metro Domestic Apps)
- Exclusively Mumbai/Delhi/Bengaluru — zero Kerala footprint
- Covers light domestic tasks only; not skilled trades
- 10-minute model requires dense worker clusters — not viable in Kerala's dispersed topology

### Nayakam (Kerala-specific)
- Kerala-native; handles electricians, plumbers, painters, carpenters
- Very small scale (36 Google Play reviews); limited geographic reach
- No half-day booking; doesn't cover coconut climbers or daily migrant construction workers

### Hello Nariyal / Coconut Development Board
- Government-backed phone/WhatsApp call centre; 1,924 registered climbers; ₹50/tree
- No mobile app, no real-time availability, no ratings, coconut-only

### WhatsApp / Word-of-Mouth (dominant today)
- Requires pre-existing social capital; unscalable
- No verification, rating, or accountability
- Workers cannot be discovered outside existing social graph

---

## What's Missing in the Market

1. **No platform serving Kerala's niche informal trades** — coconut climbers, daily cement construction workers, half-day masons not served by any app
2. **No daily-wage / half-day booking model** — every platform prices by task or hour; Kerala labour market is organised around the full day / half day
3. **No supply-side organisation for migrant workers** — 2.5M migrant workers are invisible to digital platforms
4. **Urban Company's worker exploitation creates an opening** — zero-joining-fee, lower-commission model would be highly attractive to displaced supply
5. **Geographic coverage blind spots** — Urban Company only in Kochi + Trivandrum; Kozhikode, Thrissur, Palakkad, Kannur, Kottayam unserved

- Quora: "Why is it difficult to get a plumber or electrician in Kerala?" ranks organically
- 900+ plumber WhatsApp group link aggregator sites — makeshift discovery channels
- 2024 Sage Journals research: construction worker shortages in Kerala affecting wages and project delivery
- 1,300+ RERA projects + 133,000 homes under Life Mission — sustained demand
- Al Jazeera 2024: Urban Company worker exploitation → workers looking for alternatives

## Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Cold-start two-sided marketplace | High | High | Ernakulam-only pilot; in-person worker onboarding |
| Cash-first workers, digital literacy | Medium-High | High | WhatsApp notification fallback; Malayalam UI; in-person onboarding |
| Regulatory / labour law uncertainty | Medium | Medium | Structure workers as service providers; BOCW compatibility |
| Urban Company Tier-2 expansion | Medium | Medium | Our worker categories (coconut climbers, migrant masons) outside their model |
| Trust / safety incidents | Medium | Medium | Aadhaar verification + visible ratings in MVP |
| Seasonal demand volatility | Low-Medium | Low | Demand forecasting; proactive pre-season recruitment |

---

## Key Takeaways for the PRD

- [ ] **Build for Kerala's specific worker categories** — name them explicitly; don't defer
- [ ] **Booking unit must be the day (or half-day)** — not task or hour
- [ ] **Worker onboarding must be zero-cost and WhatsApp-compatible**
- [ ] **Launch hyperlocally in Ernakulam** — 20+ verified workers per skill before opening
- [ ] **Worker identity verification is non-negotiable in MVP** — Aadhaar + photo + post-job rating
